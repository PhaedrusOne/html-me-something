Types of Betta Fish

If you ever happen to find yourself in a petshop. Most likely you will come across these beautifully colored fish in a small plastic container. Sometimes called Siamese fighting fish(Betta splendens) are one of the most popular freshwater pet fish. Native to Thailand, Camboida and Vietnam, the males are extremely aggressive and territorial. Bettas come in a multitude of color and fin shapes.

  • Crowntail Betta
  • Halfmoon Betta
  • Veiltail Betta
  • Plakat Betta

Crowntail Betta

Crowntail Bettas are named after its Crown shaped caudal fins. Life span of a Crown Betta is 2-3 years. The size is usually 3" and mostly come in blue and red.

Half Moon Betta

Half Moon Bettas are named after the shape there fins make when flared. They are carnivores and live on a diet of bloodworms, dried shrimp and live counterparts.

Veiltail Betta

Veiltail Bettas are also named after there long flowing tail. Veiltails along with all other Betta fish breathe through there gills and from there Labyrinth organ which allows them to take gulps of air from the surface.

Plakat Betta

Plakat Bettas are short finned allowing them to move swiftly. Plakats are less commonly found in petshops. Unlike other long finned Bettas, Plakats were bred to be fighting fish in Southeast Asia.
